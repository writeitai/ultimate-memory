# D65 Binding Package — Adversarial Review

The package gets the high-level decision right and preserves the two reconciled divergences, but it is **not yet implementation-ready**. The largest problem is that the prose binds immutable representation generations while the binding storage/schema model still has only one mutable conversion result per document version at a content-hash-only path; the related provenance, disclosure, and lifecycle fields likewise have no occurrence-grain home. A cold implementer would have to invent the missing identity model and would produce mutually incompatible implementations. The locator union, deterministic disclosure mapping, mounted deep-link behavior, media-index capability model, and acceptance checks also contain material gaps or contradictions. These are translation defects from the synthesis into binding text, not reasons to reopen the settled conceptual choices.

1. **SEVERITY: must-fix**  
   **LOCATION:** `plan/designs/media_design.md` §1 and §6; `plan/designs/e0_files_design.md` §2–§3; `plan/designs/postgres_schema_design.md` §6 (`document_versions`); `plan/designs/evidence_lifecycle_design.md` §1/§6  
   **ISSUE:** The synthesis adopted “representation generations as identified immutable objects,” but the binding package never models such an object. `media_design.md` says old representations remain immutable, yet artifacts are still addressed as `<doc_id>/<content_hash>/document.md`, `document_versions` has one `markdown_uri`/`conversion_uri`/converter stamp, and the lineage has no `current_representation_id`. Re-converting unchanged bytes under a new ASR/VLM therefore either overwrites the old coordinate system or requires an implementer to invent an unbound storage/schema layer. This also contradicts historical grounding, replay-from-storage, and the lifecycle eval’s requirement that every old `document.md`, source map, and block sidecar remain resolvable. `content_objects` additionally still says identical bytes are “converted once,” which is false once one byte object may own multiple converter generations.  
   **PROPOSED FIX:** Bind a `document_representations` (or equivalent) immutable object keyed by `representation_id`/output hash, belonging to a `version_id`, with route/config/component identity, manifest and output hashes, artifact URIs, status, and creation time. Put the representation ID/hash in artifact paths, point each document version at its current representation, keep prior generations addressable, and switch the current pointer only after conversion→E1→E2 completes. Update the content-object and conversion-reuse wording accordingly.

2. **SEVERITY: must-fix**  
   **LOCATION:** `plan/designs/evidence_lifecycle_design.md` §1, §3, and §6; `plan/designs/media_design.md` §6; `decisions.md` D54 refinement and D56; `plan/designs/postgres_schema_design.md` §§6–8  
   **ISSUE:** The claimed “full representation generation” basis is not full and is internally inconsistent. It is defined as `(content_hash, converter_version, blockizer_version, extractor_version)`, while the same lifecycle design and D56 say `structurer_version` is in `extraction_input_hash` and a structurer bump is an extraction boundary because section roles affect Selection. A structurer-only bump can therefore mint new claims without changing the stated D54 basis, leaving no formal trigger to retire the prior claim generation. The tuple also conflates three different things: source-version identity, representation identity, and extraction identity; `extractor_version` is not part of a representation. No stored basis/representation coordinate exists on chunks, claims, currency events, or reconciliation records.  
   **PROPOSED FIX:** Define the identities separately. For example: source snapshot = `version_id`; representation = `representation_id` covering converter output and provenance semantics; extraction basis = `(representation_id, blockizer_version, structurer_version, extractor_version)` (or explicitly include blockizer/structure inside representation identity). Persist that coordinate on the occurrence/currency records and define completion/atomic-swap behavior. Reconcile D54, D56, lifecycle §6, and the lifecycle eval to the same definition.

3. **SEVERITY: must-fix**  
   **LOCATION:** `plan/designs/media_design.md` §4; `plan/designs/e1_chunks_design.md` §2; `decisions.md` D57 refinement/D65(4); `plan/implementation_evals/eval_checks/media_source_locators.yaml`  
   **ISSUE:** The `SourceLocator` contract is incomplete and loses an adopted synthesis item. SYNTHESIS §2 explicitly includes a pageless `source_range`, but both binding copies omit it and instead make HTML/email/plain-source mapping nullable. The displayed union does not contain or define inheritance of `version_id`, `content_hash`, or the raw handle despite saying every locator is version-pinned; `video_region` has no `precision` although the eval requires every variant to carry it; “declared time base” has no field or global definition; and `region`, `track`, `keyframe`, page numbering, interval inclusivity, coordinate origin, and multi-locator spans are undefined. The eval and design therefore disagree even before implementation.  
   **PROPOSED FIX:** Publish one normative discriminated schema and reference it everywhere. Restore `source_range`; make the immutable raw/version/representation handle explicit (or specify one precise enclosing object that supplies it); give every variant a defined precision enum; define normalized rectangle coordinates, page base, end-bound semantics, track IDs, `keyframe_id`, and milliseconds relative to a named media timeline. Specify that a source-map entry and a claim may map to one or more locators and how intersections/merges are resolved. Make the eval use exactly that schema.

4. **SEVERITY: must-fix**  
   **LOCATION:** `plan/designs/media_design.md` §2 and §5; `decisions.md` D65(2)/(5); `plan/implementation_evals/eval_checks/media_converter_routes_bound.yaml` and `media_derivation_disclosure.yaml`  
   **ISSUE:** The promised deterministic `evidence_mode` inheritance is not realizable from the prescribed section shape. `## Visual description` and `## Shot notes` can naturally contain both observations (“Alice enters”) and interpretations (“Alice looks hesitant”), but the design labels a whole section once and forbids any downstream/per-claim judgment. There is no required `Model interpretation` section, no rule constraining a section to one mode, and no rule for a claim spanning differently labelled ranges. Consequently the example that must surface `model_interpretation` cannot be derived deterministically from the route shape as written.  
   **PROPOSED FIX:** Bind mode-homogeneous converter ranges/blocks, not merely headings named by derivation kind. Define the route-to-`derivation_kind`/`evidence_mode` mapping, require interpretations to be emitted in a separate labelled section/range from observations, and define mixed-span handling (split the claim, attach multiple labelled supports, or reject it). Add an eval fixture containing observation and interpretation in the same source so deterministic separation is tested rather than asserted.

5. **SEVERITY: must-fix**  
   **LOCATION:** `plan/designs/media_design.md` §4–§6; `plan/designs/retrieval_design.md` §5; `plan/designs/postgres_schema_design.md` §§7–9 (`chunk_claims`, `claims`, `grounding_audits`, `relation_evidence`, `observation_evidence`)  
   **ISSUE:** The binding schema has no home for the newly bound provenance and disclosure. The prose says labels are “cached on the evidence link,” but neither fact-evidence table has them, `claims` has no representation/derivation/source-locator fields, and `chunk_claims` has no representation coordinate. This is not safely claim-grain: an identical transcript may reuse one claim across representation generations while only timestamps, speaker labels, model family, or source map changed. Those are occurrence-specific facts. The response envelope then makes the loss worse by placing one singular `derivation` beside a flat `source_locators[]` on a result, even though a fact may have many evidence claims with different modes, models, generations, and locators. The association between locator, derivation, and supporting claim is destroyed.  
   **PROPOSED FIX:** Bind occurrence-grain provenance, using `chunk_claims` or a dedicated claim-grounding/evidence-occurrence table keyed by claim + representation occurrence. Store/query `representation_id`/derivation run, source modality, derivation kind, evidence mode, model family, and locator set there. Make audits name the exact occurrence/raw target they judged. Change the envelope to an `evidence[]`/provenance-item list where each claim/evidence handle owns its derivation record and locators. Define how current-testimony hydration selects the current occurrence and how historical hydration selects the old one.

6. **SEVERITY: must-fix**  
   **LOCATION:** `plan/designs/media_design.md` §4 and §8; `plan/designs/e0_files_design.md` §5–§6; `plan/designs/retrieval_design.md` §3 and §7; `plan/analysis/retrieval_scenarios.md` S59  
   **ISSUE:** The P3/mounted half of the merged package was not carried into the actual P3 contract. `media_design.md` binds media stubs with current version, raw locator/pointer, and previews, but E0’s normative stub frontmatter still lists only `doc_id`, `artifact_uri`, `content_hash`, and `section_path`; its P3 layout and navigation ladder mention neither raw locators nor previews. Also, `original.mp3#t=873` is a media-URL fragment, not a filesystem seek operation: a mounted coding harness opening that literal path will not find a file, and no mounted helper/client behavior is specified. S59 nevertheless requires a mounted agent to “seek via the deep link.” The unmounted API path is described, but its `hydrate` table signature still accepts only IDs and depth, not a locator.  
   **PROPOSED FIX:** Update E0 §5–§6 as part of the binding package: define media-stub fields, preview links/materialization, immutable version/representation raw handles, and no per-keyframe pseudo-documents. Specify mounted behavior concretely—e.g. the fragment is display metadata and the skill invokes a named local seek command against the mounted raw path plus `start_ms/end_ms`, or ship the same `source_open` helper on both surfaces. Give `hydrate` a normative locator parameter or bind a separate `source_open` signature, including spatial-region response semantics.

7. **SEVERITY: should-fix**  
   **LOCATION:** SYNTHESIS §2 adopted points 4, 5, and 9; `plan/designs/media_design.md` §2 and §10; `plan/designs/e0_files_design.md` §3  
   **ISSUE:** The generalized manifest/route contract is weaker than the adopted package. The binding manifest requires route, models, versions, and section labels, but drops coverage/gap reporting, warnings, output hashes, and a binding execution-context record. Those fields are necessary for adaptive video sampling honesty, representation identity/replay, corrupt or unsupported intervals, and D61 provider/privacy audit. Execution context appears only in a spike. The adopted example section shape also named acoustic events and regions, while the bound routes expose only transcript/visual description/visible text/shot notes.  
   **PROPOSED FIX:** Make manifest output hashes, producer/component graph, execution context, selected tracks, coverage policy/result, unsupported/corrupt gaps, warnings, and derived-asset provenance required fields (with capability-nullability where appropriate). State whether `Acoustic events` and region descriptions are bound converter sections; if intentionally rejected, record that departure from SYNTHESIS rather than silently omitting it.

8. **SEVERITY: should-fix**  
   **LOCATION:** `plan/designs/media_design.md` §7; `plan/designs/retrieval_design.md` §3; `plan/requirements/requirements_v3.md` P1; `plan/implementation_evals/eval_checks/media_p1_media_segments.yaml`  
   **ISSUE:** `media_segments` is bound as one Lance table with one “CLIP-class” media embedder spanning text, images, audio, and video, but the docs do not define compatibility when visual and acoustic encoders use different vector spaces/dimensions. CLIP itself does not establish the audio contract implied by the prose. There is only an all-or-nothing “no media embedder” boundary, not a capability response for a deployment that supports text→image but not audio→audio. The search signature binds text/image/audio query inputs, while the eval checks only “at least text queries,” dropping image→image and audio-input behavior from acceptance despite the synthesis explicitly separating those tasks.  
   **PROPOSED FIX:** Define `media_segments` as a logical target over capability/version-specific subindexes (or bind one truly joint model requirement), with modality, embedding family/version/dimension, representation ID, and locator in each row. Advertise supported query→target modality pairs and return typed boundaries per missing pair; fuse incompatible subindex rankings through RRF, never raw vector scores. Extend the eval to configured image and audio query inputs and to partial-capability negatives.

9. **SEVERITY: must-fix**  
   **LOCATION:** `plan/implementation_evals/eval_checks/e0_converter_router_versioned.yaml`; all five new `media_*.yaml` checks  
   **ISSUE:** A pre-existing acceptance check now directly contradicts D65: `e0_converter_router_versioned` still requires `document.md + page map + media[]`, so an implementation of the new `source_map + derived_assets[] + manifest` contract cannot make the exhaustive check suite pass. The new checks also encode unresolved contradictions/omissions: `media_source_locators` requires precision on `video_region` but omits the adopted `source_range` and says the source map is persisted “per version” rather than per immutable representation; `media_lifecycle_representation_basis` checks old-generation immutability but not the representation object/current pointer/representation-addressed paths/atomic switch that make it possible; `media_derivation_disclosure` demands total passthrough labels on non-media routes although the binding text only inconsistently implies that scope; and `media_p1_media_segments` under-tests the bound image/audio query surface.  
   **PROPOSED FIX:** Update the older converter check in this round. After resolving findings 1–8, make all five media checks quote the same normative schemas and add targeted tests/observable conditions for immutable representation storage and switchover, occurrence-grain provenance association, mode-homogeneous inheritance, source-range locators, mounted/unmounted seek behavior, and every configured media-search direction. Either explicitly bind total derivation labels for all routes or remove that over-binding from the disclosure check.

10. **SEVERITY: should-fix**  
   **LOCATION:** `plan/designs/media_design.md` §5, §9, and §10; `plan/implementation_evals/eval_checks/media_derivation_disclosure.yaml` condition 5  
   **ISSUE:** Rule 2 is violated by leaving derivation-family correlation for “any future confidence policy/math.” This is deferred policy, not a stated non-goal, and the eval repeats the deferral. The synthesis also explicitly left stricter K3 treatment of high-impact `model_observation` as an open policy spike, but that spike disappeared from §10. Merely retaining provenance does not say what the complete system does with correlated model errors. “A later, better diarization” is lifecycle intent rather than phasing, but should be phrased as “any changed diarization generation” to avoid the same ambiguity.  
   **PROPOSED FIX:** Bind the current policy now (for example: lineage counts remain the only confidence input and derivation family is disclosure-only) and state correlation adjustment as an explicit non-goal/documented alternative, or design a correlation-aware policy. Restore the high-impact K3 admission question as a measured policy spike if it remains genuinely open; do not call an unspecified future policy part of the design.

11. **SEVERITY: should-fix**  
   **LOCATION:** `decisions.md` D65 and the D54/D56/D57 refinement history  
   **ISSUE:** D65 is not self-contained relative to the merged eight-point package. It announces “Seven bindings” and omits the eighth binding—P3 media stubs + previews only, no raw copies and no per-keyframe pseudo-documents—from the Decision paragraph. More importantly, it reduces the adopted immutable representation-generation object to a four-string tuple. D56 is not annotated even though representation-generation identity, converter-upgrade exact reuse, timestamp-only changes, and occurrence attachment materially refine its reuse contract; D65’s own interaction table says D54–D56 are affected.  
   **PROPOSED FIX:** Add the eighth P3 binding to D65, state the representation object/current-pointer semantics rather than only a tuple, and add a D56 refinement note covering representation-aware reuse and occurrence provenance. Keep the existing D32, D38, D49, D51, D54, and D57 notes; their placement and broad direction are otherwise correct.

12. **SEVERITY: should-fix**  
   **LOCATION:** Untouched `plan/designs/overall_design.md` §2 and §4; `plan/designs/postgres_schema_design.md` decision-coverage/schema sections  
   **ISSUE:** The top-level binding inventory still carries pre-D65 assumptions. Overall §2 says GCS artifacts are rebuilt by rerunning E0 under `converter_version`, conflicting with D7 replay and the newly required preservation of old nondeterministic representations, and lists P1 only as chunks/claims/relation labels, omitting `media_segments`. The canonical schema’s DDL and decision map do not mention D65 beyond one `conversion_uri` comment, despite D65 requiring new authoritative representation/provenance state. These omissions make the “current” design index point implementers back to the old architecture.  
   **PROPOSED FIX:** Update the store table and E0/P1 pipeline summary for immutable representation generations and `media_segments`; add D65 to the schema coverage map and make the actual DDL changes required by findings 1 and 5. Distinguish replaying stored nondeterministic output from regenerating a rebuildable projection.

## Checked and found sound

- The canonical-text correction is clear in `media_design.md`, E0 §2, and D65: transcript testimony belongs in `document.md`; `.vtt`/JSON under `media/` is interchange only.
- The three top-level media routes, conservative unresolved-speaker behavior, raw-original/derived-asset split, and “media is E0, not a new plane” faithfully preserve the synthesis.
- The processing-driven lifecycle outcome is consistent wherever stated: converter upgrades do not add lineage count, non-rederivation raises `support_withdrawn`, and source retraction is not inferred.
- The D32 modality-aware audit rationale is correctly carried into E2/E3 and D32’s refinement note: auditing generated Markdown alone would grade the converter against itself.
- The direct-media-search decision is present rather than deferred; `media_segments`, RRF fusion, zero-LLM query behavior, rebuildability, and typed capability-boundary intent all appear in the binding docs.
- S59 is materially strengthened, and S62/S63 exercise the two missing discovery/grounding cases. The S1–S63 range updates are consistent.
- The eval inventory is numerically correct: 81 YAML checks exist and exactly five are `media_*` checks. README, questions, overall-design index row, and D1–D65 range bumps agree.
- The D32, D38, D49, D51, D54, and D57 refinement notes are attached to the right historical decisions and accurately summarize their intended D65 direction, subject to the mechanical gaps above.
